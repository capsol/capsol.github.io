Prototype

The marriage of graphene and porous silicon for supercapacitors provide a superior long-term alternative to energy storage in solar cells. While solar panels can last with conventional power sources, they lack storage and waste extra energy. So far, deep-cycle batteries have been used, but they possess disadvantages. Some batteries have a "memory effect”, in which they lose the ability to sustain a charge over a long period of time. This clearly becomes a problem when desiring long-lasting energy containment and production. They also harbor severe environmental concerns and thus are not a considerable alternative in the long-run. Graphene's high energy density, relatively small size, and minimal impact on the ecosystem, fused with the simplicity and conductive powers of porous silicon, provides a cleaner and more elegant solution to the dilemma of solar power.

Impact

In the future, graphene-silicon-based supercapacitors will be able to absorb energy faster, store larger amounts of energy, and release energy faster than a lithium-ion battery will be able to. Despite the lack of efficiency of solar cells, prolonged use of the supercapacitor allows the solar cell to absorb and produce more usable energy, turning them into the most viable and sustainable alternative to other sources of electrical power.

CapSol will replace current solar cell energy storage units like lithium-ion batteries, nickel-cadmium batteries, and lead-acid batteries. CapSol’s unique design allows it to hold more energy, charge and release energy faster, and degrade slower than most batteries. The electricity released by solar arrays will be brought to a storage facility with multiple CapSol units placed in parallel lines. It will allow the electricity to be released at a moment’s notice, transforming solar energy from a slightly useful supplement of energy to a major source for the world’s power.

The development of CapSol will facilitate the transition from lithium-ion batteries to supercapacitors in all electrical devices. Graphene supercapacitors will be developed for smartphones, electric cars, computers, and other everyday household items. Devices will charge in seconds and retain a larger amount of charge, eradicating inconveniences like low battery life and long periods of charge. Electric cars will become an increasingly viable alternative to gasoline-powered cars, reduce greenhouse gas emissions significantly and help in the fight against global warming. Furthermore, while conventional, toxic batteries are gradually removed from landfills, biodegradable supercapacitors put a halt to clogged landfills inundated with conventional batteries by providing a new alternative. Hazards such as explosion, leakage of toxic chemicals, environmental damage, and disposal challenges are all greatly reduced, if not eliminated, by the introduction of the cleaner and superior alternative.
