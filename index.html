Experiments performed on graphene have demonstrated its potential for energy storage. When combined with porous silicon, a material with an equal level of potential for use in supercapacitors, the possibilities for a supercapacitor that could surpass batteries becomes very real.

While current solar panels can last with conventional power sources, they lack storage and waste extra energy. Graphene's high energy density, and minimal impact on the ecosystem, fused with porous silicon, provide a cleaner and more elegant solution to the dilemma of solar power.

CapSol is a new tech that serves as energy storage for future solar power collectors.The fusion of both silicon and graphene in CapSol has an energy density two orders of magnitude greater than commercially-available pure silicon or pure graphene supercapacitors, and is a more elegant and cleaner solution in the long run.

History of Graphene and Solar Energy

home Previous Next

CapSol will replace current solar cell energy storage units i.e. batteries. The electricity released by solar arrays will be brought to a storage facility with multiple CapSol units placed in parallel lines, allowing the electricity to be released at a moment’s notice.

With the large amount of storage supercapacitors have, many electronic devices can last much longer than with current lithium ion batteries. We can use supercapacitors to create electronic charging stations, for fast and efficient energy transfers.

The development of CapSol will facilitate the transition from batteries to supercapacitors in all electrical devices. Devices will charge in seconds and retain a larger amount of charge, eradicating inconveniences like low battery life and long periods of charge. Environmental hazards will be reduced and perhaps even eliminated, once CapSol is implemented into our lives.
